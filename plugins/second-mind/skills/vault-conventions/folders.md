# Folders

Per-folder reference. What each folder is for, what goes in, what doesn't.

## 00-inbox/

**Purpose.** Capture zone — anything written when deciding where it belongs would slow you down.

**Processed on cadence.** Typically weekly. Each item gets promoted to its proper home, linked into an existing note, or deleted.

## 10-atoms/

**Purpose.** Flat collection of portable one-idea notes.

**Flat.** No subfolders. This is load-bearing. Atoms find their neighbors through links and maps, not through filesystem proximity.

**One idea per note, named for its content** — `vertx-5-verticlebase-migration.md`, not `java-notes-3.md`.

**Not atoms:** bodies of work (library items), indexes (maps), notes that require their originating context to make sense.

Extraction mechanics: see `operations.md`.

## 20-maps/

**Purpose.** Navigation hubs — curated entry points into the graph.

**What lives here.** Topic MOCs, area hubs, client hubs, reading lists, idea indexes, the active-work list. Anything that points at things without containing them.

**Two flavors.**

- *Curated* — hand-maintained lists of wikilinks. Use when ordering matters (a reading path, a thematic cluster).
- *Queried* — generated by Dataview from frontmatter (e.g. `status: active`, items with a deadline).

**Map type in frontmatter,** not in subfolders. Keeps the taxonomy flexible.

**When to create one.** Roughly 5+ related notes where a reading order or mental model would help. Below that, direct links between notes are enough.

**Maps don't contain working material.** They point at things. Work happens in atoms, library items, or the journal.

## 30-journal/

**Purpose.** Daily and meeting notes. Timestamped records.

**Naming.** Daily notes: `2026-04-18.md`. Meeting notes: `2026-04-18-topic.md`.

**Meeting workflow.** Capture freely during; after, extract durable material into `10-atoms/` and link back. The meeting note stays as source record; the atom becomes reusable.

**Ephemera** (shopping lists, packing lists) can attach to daily notes or live in a single scratchpad in `99-meta/`. Don't build a system for disposable content.

## 40-library/

**Purpose.** Bodies of work engaged with across time.

**Library items are folders.** One per body of work, named descriptively. Each contains its own working material.

**Status is metadata, not folder location.** Frontmatter carries `status: active | dormant | finished | reference`, `deadline:` when applicable, `context:` when it matters. Items don't migrate between primary folders — only metadata and map membership change over time. The `active.md` map curates what's currently under attention.

**When content inside a library item becomes portable,** it extracts to an atom (see `operations.md`). The library item keeps its own life; atoms persist across contexts.

**New library items come from** the startup link-creation flow (generic agent) or a body of work taking shape in conversation (brainstorming agent).

## 80-claude/

**Purpose.** The brainstorming agent's private workspace — memory, scratch, open threads.

- **Brainstorming agent:** see `claude-space.md` for operational details.
- **Any other agent:** off-limits. No read, no write.

## 90-graveyard/

**Purpose.** Abandoned content. Things the user won't return to but won't delete.

**One-way and write-only.** No maps point here. If something in the graveyard turns out to matter, extract the valuable part to a new atom — don't treat the graveyard as a second library.

**Distinction from library.** About the user's relationship to the material, not its state. A finished essay still consulted stays in library; a finished essay the user is done with moves to graveyard.

## 99-meta/

**Purpose.** Vault-about-vault.

**Contents.** Conventions, templates, the structure-decisions log, general scratchpad. `vault-structure.md` lives here — load it directly when a structural question comes up.

**Structure-decisions log.** Every structural change gets an entry with date, change, and reason. The reasoning matters more than the outcome.

## assets/

**Purpose.** Attachments. Images, PDFs, audio, anything binary.

**Flat, at vault root.** Obsidian is configured to drop new attachments here automatically. Don't subfolder by topic — attachments are found through the notes that embed them.

**Exception.** A library item with many attachments can have its own `attachments/` subfolder to stay self-contained.
